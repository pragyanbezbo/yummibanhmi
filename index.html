Câu chuyện · Our story

No market research. Just a backpack and an obsession.

Yum Mi Bánh Mì started the way most sensible businesses do not. We went backpacking across Vietnam, ate our body weight in bánh mì, drank phin coffee on tiny plastic stools, and came home unable to think about anything else.

So we set up a small counter near Gauhati University and started baking. The baguettes are made fresh, the pickles are made in-house, and the coffee drips through a metal phin one cup at a time. No machines doing the thinking for us.

We keep prices low on purpose. Street food is everyday food, and we want it to be your everyday too. Not a treat. A habit.

Find us under Outpost 7 Cafe in Satmile, every evening except Wednesday.

Thực đơn · The menu

A small menu, done properly.

Everything below is made to order. Ask at the counter for today's fillings.

Từ bếp · From the kitchen

Bánh Mì

The signature baguette

Crackly baguette, pâté and butter, đồ chua pickles, cucumber, coriander and chilli. Pick your filling at the counter.

Gỏi Cuốn

Fresh summer rolls

Soft rice paper rolls packed with vermicelli, crunchy veg and herbs. Served cold with our house dipping sauce.

Từ phin · From the phin

Cà Phê Phin

Vietnamese drip coffee

Dark roast dripped slowly through a metal phin filter. Hot and black, or iced over condensed milk.

Bạc Xỉu

Mostly milk, a little coffee

Sweet, gentle and dangerously easy to finish. The Saigon classic for people who claim they don't like coffee.

Cà Phê Muối

Salted coffee

Huế's famous trick. A whipped salted cream that somehow makes the coffee underneath taste sweeter.

Cà Phê Trứng

Egg coffee

Hanoi in a cup. A cloud of whipped egg yolk custard floating on strong, dark coffee.

Can't decide?  Start with the bánh mì and a cà phê sữa đá. That pairing is the reason this café exists.
